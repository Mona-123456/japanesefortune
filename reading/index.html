What a Four Pillars reading tells you

Four Pillars of Destiny — Shichū Suimei (四柱推命) in Japanese, BaZi in Chinese — reads your birth moment as eight classical characters. This free reading gives you three of the core layers.

Your Day Master is the heavenly stem of the day you were born: the elemental character the tradition reads as you. There are ten of them — the tall tree, the vine, the sun, the candle, the mountain, the field, the axe, the jewel, the river, the fog. Ten types →

Your five-element balance counts the Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal and Water across your whole chart. Some charts run hot, some run heavy, some are unusually even — and tradition reads each shape as both a gift and an assignment.

Your four animals come from the earthly branches of your year, month, day and hour. Most people know only their year animal. You have more than one. The twelve animals →

Why the year begins in February

In this tradition the year does not turn on January 1st. It turns at Risshun (立春), the astronomical start of spring, around February 4th — the day after Setsubun, when Japanese households throw beans to drive the demons out.

This matters more than it sounds. If you were born in January or early February, your chart may belong to the previous year. A reading that ignores this boundary will hand you the wrong pillars entirely. Ours calculates the solar terms astronomically, and — if you tell us where you were born — corrects for apparent solar time. How Four Pillars works →

What this is, and what it isn't

A chart is traditionally read as a map of tendencies, never a verdict. The recurring metaphor in the old texts is weather: you cannot choose the climate you were born into, but knowing it changes how you sail.

Take what resonates and leave the rest. Nothing here is medical, legal, or financial advice, and nothing here is a reason to postpone your life.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need my exact birth time?

No. Without a time, the hour pillar is omitted — but your Day Master, your five-element balance and three of your four animals are all calculated from the date alone. If you do know your birth time, the reading gains a fourth pillar.

What if I was born in January or early February?

Then your chart may belong to the previous year. The traditional year begins at Risshun in early February, not on January 1st, and this reading uses the astronomical boundary rather than the calendar one.

Is this Chinese BaZi or Japanese Shichū Suimei?

The same underlying system — it travelled from China to Japan over a thousand years ago and developed its own vocabulary and readings there. This site works in the Japanese tradition: Japanese stem and branch names, Japanese readings, and the Japanese folklore attached to each sign.

Is my birth data sent anywhere?

No. The entire calculation runs in your browser. Nothing about your birth date leaves your device, and we do not store it.
