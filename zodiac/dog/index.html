The Dog (戌): The Loyal Guardian — Your Zodiac Animal

You would take the hit for people who never noticed you were standing in front of them.

Element: Earth · Hour: 7pm–9pm · Direction: West-northwest · Season: Late autumn

Who the Dog is

The eleventh branch (戌, inu) — loyalty, honesty, and a protector's instinct. It belongs to early evening, the direction west-northwest, and the Earth of late autumn: the ground closing down for winter.

The Dog is read as faithful, just, and dependable — the sign that keeps its word and expects you to keep yours.

The guardian at the gate

The stone dogs guarding every Japanese shrine — the komainu — are the Dog's cultural echo: the loyal beast that stands at the threshold and keeps evil out. And the Dog is woven into Japanese life at its most tender point: the inu no hi, the "Day of the Dog," when pregnant women visit shrines for the obi-iwai blessing, because dogs were believed to give birth easily. The Dog is the sign of the guardian — of the gate, of the vulnerable, of the ones who cannot guard themselves.

The Dog's nature

Fidelity. Dog people are associated with loyalty, fairness, and a strong moral compass. The shadow the texts name is anxiety — the guardian who cannot stop scanning for threats, the cynic who has been let down and now expects to be.

Time, direction, and season

The hour of 7pm to 9pm — early evening, the day winding down. Direction west-northwest. Late autumn, Earth again, the pause before winter.

Who the Dog moves with

Harmony in the Fire trinity with Tiger and Horse (寅午戌). Opposition to the Dragon (辰) — the loyalist against the visionary.

The Year of the Dog

Read as a year of loyalty, fairness, and community — a grounded year favouring honesty, duty, and looking after each other.

The Dog is one of the twelve Earthly Branches — the ox, the tiger, the dragon, and the rest.

Frequently asked questions

What does the Dog mean in the Japanese zodiac?

The Dog (戌, inu) is the eleventh Earthly Branch — Earth element, early evening, west-northwest, late autumn. It signifies loyalty, honesty, and protection.

How is the Dog connected to Japanese custom?

The guardian komainu at shrines echo the Dog, and pregnant women visit shrines on the "Day of the Dog" for a safe-birth blessing, as dogs were thought to birth easily.

What is the Dog most compatible with?

From whole charts. The trinities place the Dog in a "fire frame" with the Tiger and Horse, opposite the Dragon.
